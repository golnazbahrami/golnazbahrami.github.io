Working Papers

Research

1
Joint Work
When Locking In Biodiversity Locks Up Land
with Matthew Gustafson and Eva Steiner
2026
This paper examines how conservation policies aimed at protecting biodiversity affect land use, property values, and local economic activity. Using variation in conservation easement adoption, we identify the causal effects of restricting land development on surrounding real estate markets and community welfare.
2
Joint Work
Environmental Certainty and Homeowner Investment: Evidence from Conservation Easements
with Matthew Gustafson and Eva Steiner
2026
We study how environmental certainty affects homeowner investment decisions. Exploiting quasi-random variation in the placement of conservation easements, we show that reducing uncertainty about future land use near residential properties increases homeowner willingness to invest in property improvements, consistent with theories of option value and real investment under uncertainty.
3
Joint Work
Burning Signals: Wildfire Hazard Zones and Housing Market Reactions
with Pouya Arab
2026
We examine how the designation of wildfire hazard zones affects housing markets. Using discontinuities in the mapping process, we estimate the causal impact of hazard zone classifications on home prices, insurance costs, and buyer behavior, with implications for climate risk pricing in residential real estate.
4
Joint Work
Passive Debt Ownership and Corporate Financial Policy
with Brian Gibbons
2026
We investigate how the rise of passive investing in corporate debt markets affects firm financial policy. Passive ownership of corporate bonds may weaken creditor monitoring, alter covenant enforcement, and change the cost of external finance — with downstream effects on leverage, investment, and risk-taking.
5
Joint Work
Environmental Risk in Municipal Bond Markets: The Role of Air Pollution
with Mansoor Shekarian
2025
We study how local air pollution exposure affects municipal bond yields and credit spreads. Municipalities in high-pollution areas face higher borrowing costs, consistent with investors pricing environmental risk into public debt. The effect is stronger for bonds issued by municipalities with greater fiscal dependence on pollution-intensive industries.
6
Solo-Authored
Extreme Weather, Household Relocation, and Real Estate Decisions
Golnaz Bahrami
2026
I examine how exposure to extreme weather events affects household migration decisions and real estate markets. Using detailed household-level data linked to weather shocks, I estimate the causal effect of climate events on residential relocation, property devaluation, and long-run regional population dynamics.
